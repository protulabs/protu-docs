Team Intelligence gives you a complete view of how your team works together — from behavioural patterns to interpersonal dynamics and what-if planning.

## Team Composition

Team Composition shows how your team's behavioural patterns combine across all 10 markers.

### Reading the visualisation

The visualisation shows:
- **Team average** across each marker
- **Individual positions** showing where each person sits
- **Coverage** indicating how well the team covers each dimension

### Interpreting patterns

**Strengths** — Markers where multiple team members score highly indicate team strengths. These are areas where your team has natural capability.

**Gaps** — Markers with low coverage may indicate gaps. Consider whether these gaps matter for your team's work, and whether to address them through hiring or development.

**Diversity** — Teams benefit from diverse perspectives. Look for markers where team members have different scores, as this can lead to richer discussions and better decisions.

## Team Dynamics

Team Dynamics shows how each team member influences the team and how they relate to each other. While Team Composition shows what patterns exist, Team Dynamics shows how people interact. It reveals who anchors the team, who bridges different groups, and how influence flows.

### Reading the visualisation

The visualisation positions each team member based on their influence style:
- **Position** reflects their role in team dynamics
- **Connections** show relationships between members
- **Clusters** reveal natural working groups

### Key patterns

**Anchors** — Some people anchor the team, providing stability and grounding. They often sit near the centre of the visualisation.

**Bridges** — Bridges connect different parts of the team. They help information flow between groups and can translate between different working styles.

**Clusters** — Natural working groups form based on complementary styles. Understanding these clusters helps with project assignments and communication.

## Scenario views

Scenario views let you explore how your team would change if you added or removed members. Use scenarios to test hypothetical changes before making them.

### Creating a scenario

1. Open Team Intelligence for your team
2. Click **Add to scenario** on a person or candidate
3. The visualisation updates to show the projected impact

### Scenario types

**Adding someone** — See how a candidate or employee from another team would fit. The visualisation shows their projected position and how existing dynamics would shift.

**Removing someone** — Understand the impact of someone leaving. See which gaps would open and how the remaining team would need to adapt.

### Using scenarios

Scenarios help you:
- Evaluate candidates for team fit
- Plan for succession
- Understand key person dependencies
- Make informed team composition decisions
