If you've been invited to take a Protu assessment, this page walks you through what it involves, what you'll see afterwards, and how your results are used.

---

## The Assessment

### What it is

A 15-20 minute conversational experience. You'll respond to open-ended prompts about how you approach work. There are no trick questions and no right or wrong answers.

### What it measures

Protu measures **behavioural patterns**: how you work, not what you know.

| It measures | It doesn't measure |
|-------------|-------------------|
| How you approach problems | Your intelligence or IQ |
| How you work with others | Your skills or qualifications |
| Your working style tendencies | Your past performance |
| Your communication patterns | Your values or beliefs |

### Tips for taking it

- Be honest: there are no "correct" answers
- Respond naturally: don't try to guess what's expected
- Take your time, but don't overthink individual questions
- Find a quiet moment. 15-20 uninterrupted minutes works best

## Your Profile

After completing the assessment, you'll see your profile immediately. A **welcome modal** introduces what you're looking at on your first visit.

### Layout

Your profile is a hub-and-spoke layout. At the centre is your **influence style**: how you naturally affect those around you. The five styles are Catalyst, Supercharge, Optimise, Enable, and Support. These aren't ranked; each is a different way of influencing. See [Key Concepts](/getting-started/key-concepts/) for what each style means.

Below the influence style is a **profile summary**: a short paragraph describing your working style in plain language, including how you influence others and situations where you're most valuable. "Engage for" recommendations (situations where your strengths are particularly valuable) are woven into this narrative.

### Exploring Your Markers

Around the centre, your 10 behavioural markers are displayed as cards you can browse. Click any marker to open a **trait deep-dive** showing:

- **What this means:** A plain-language explanation of the dimension
- **How you express it:** How this pattern shows up in your work
- **Your contributing styles:** The top submarkers driving your score in this area

Each marker has an impact level:

| Level | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Exceptional** | Among the strongest in this dimension |
| **Standout** | Notably strong, often visible to others |
| **Established** | Solid capability, continuing to develop |
| **Developing** | Building capability, room to grow |
| **Starting** | Early stage, significant growth opportunity |

There's no "good" or "bad" pattern. Different combinations suit different roles and teams.

See [Key Concepts](/getting-started/key-concepts/) for the full marker list.

### Influences Through

The submarkers where you score notably higher than peers. These are your genuine relative strengths: how you make an impact on those around you.

## Your Detailed Insights

Your full insights page breaks down your behaviour in more depth.

### Markers Overview

Your position across all 10 behavioural dimensions is shown as an interactive bar chart:

| Marker | What it captures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Growth Mindset** | Orientation toward learning, embracing challenges, and development |
| **Leadership** | Ability to guide, influence, and inspire others |
| **Creativity** | Novel ideas, innovative thinking, and exploring possibilities |
| **Open-mindedness** | Receptiveness to different perspectives and tolerance of ambiguity |
| **Accountability** | Ownership, follow-through, and personal standards |
| **Perseverance** | Persistence, focus, and resilience under pressure |
| **Social Awareness** | Reading social dynamics, empathy, and interpersonal skills |
| **Teamwork** | Collaboration, relationship building, and group contribution |
| **Transparency** | Open communication, honesty, and information sharing |
| **Self-awareness** | Understanding own emotions, strengths, and limitations |

Each marker shows:
- **Impact level:** Where you currently sit (Exceptional to Starting)
- **Score:** The numerical value behind the level
- **Submarkers:** Click to expand and see the granular dimensions

## What Your Employer Sees

### They see

- Your behavioural profile (markers, levels, insights)
- How your patterns compare to the team
- Where you contribute uniquely

### They don't see

- Your individual assessment responses
- A ranking against colleagues
- A "pass/fail" judgement

Protu provides insights to help teams work better together, not to judge or rank individuals.

---

## Using Your Insights

### For self-awareness

- Understand your natural working style
- Recognise your genuine strengths
- Identify areas you might want to develop

### For conversations

- Share your profile with your manager to improve 1:1s
- Discuss how your style fits with team needs
- Use the "engage for" recommendations in your narrative to find high-impact opportunities

### For development

- Focus on growth areas that matter for your role
- Build on existing strengths, not just address gaps
- Use your profile as a starting point, not a final verdict

You have full control over your personal data:

- **View** your complete profile at any time
- **Ask questions** about how your data is used
- **Request correction** if something seems inaccurate
- **Request deletion** of your personal data

Start by contacting your employer. If you haven't heard back within 14 days, reach out to us directly at support@protu.io.

## Common Questions

### Can I retake the assessment?

This is managed by your employer. Contact them if you'd like to retake it.

### Who can see my profile?

Your workspace admins and owners can view your profile. Team members cannot see each other's individual profiles. Team Intelligence shows only aggregate patterns.

### Will this affect my job?

Protu provides insights, not decisions. Your employer uses your profile as one input among many. No automated decisions are made based on your assessment.

### How long is my data kept?

Your data is retained while your employer's workspace is active. You can request deletion at any time.
